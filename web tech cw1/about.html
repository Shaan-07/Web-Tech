Why do we need Cloud Computing?

Data has become the single most valuable asset in the modern business world. As a lot of news outlets like The Economist have published – “Data is not just the new oil, it is more valuable than oil” (The Economist, 2017). Datacentres across the globe contains most valuable resource available to mankind. The rapid increase in data generation and storage has raised some serious environmental concerns. Green Cloud computing has emerged as a crucial strategy to achieve the goal of net-zero future. Green cloud computing offers a range of solutions that aim to optimize resource utilization, lower energy consumption, and adapt sustainable practices throughout the cloud computing lifecycle.

What is Cloud Computing?

Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services—including servers, storage, databases, networking, software, analytics, and intelligence—over the Internet (“the cloud”) to offer faster innovation, flexible resources, and economies of scale.

What is Green Cloud Computing?

Green cloud computing is nothing but a term in the modern industry that refers to the important environmental benefits cloud computing offers to the society. The term combines two words: green, which means eco-friendly, and cloud computing. (Rodrigues, 2022)
