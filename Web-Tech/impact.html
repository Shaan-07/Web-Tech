The advantages of Cloud Computing

Reduce energy usage: An organization needs a constant power supply to operate an on-site datacenter. They also need an active cooling system to counter overheating and when the equipment is no longer usable, that creates e-waste. However, cloud computing reduces these numbers significantly. Research funded by Google and conducted by Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory in collaboration with Northwestern University found that switching common software applications to the cloud would drop energy usage by 87% (AgileIT, 2023).

Decrease greenhouse gas emissions: A normal on-site datacenter produces far more greenhouse gases than its cloud alternative. Cloud decreases the rate of carbon emissions by reducing energy requirement and consumption. A survey conducted by WSP and Accenture showed that green cloud computing has a considerable effect on carbon emissions. It showed that by using cloud technology, large companies can lessen carbon footprint by 30% (AgileIT, 2023).

Dematerialization: It means to replace the physical high-carbon products with virtual alternatives. Cloud computing removes the need for separate on-site data center for each organization. Instead, they can opt for suitable cloud services to reduce their carbon footprint and cost. Cloud computing also enables organizations to go paperless which also provides more security.

Reduce E-Waste production: Cloud computing provides all services virtually. This enables organizations to use cloud servers and database instead of building their own datacenters which results in need for less equipment. This development directly reduces E-waste resulting in contribution towards a net-zero future.

The Impact of Cloud Computing on businesses

Short-term Medium-term Long-term
1-2 Years 2-5 Years 5-10 Years
  • Organizations will adopt more visualization of their datacenters and IT infrastructure. This will reduce their energy consumption and cost.
  • Cloud datacenters will be supported by more advanced technologies such as artificial intelligence which will improve the service it provides.
  • Big tech companies such Microsoft and Amazon are already in this stage and small enterprises are adopting it. The market of cloud data is estimated to reach £864 billion by 2025.
  • More emphasis on ‘Green’ cloud computing and developing more sustainable ways to implement the model.
  • Countries across the globes are supporting green cloud computing. European Union’s Green Cloud initiative is evidence.
  • Huge investments on eco-friendly datacenters.
  • Large organizations and governments will start to adopt fully functional net-zero datacenters powered by 100% renewable energy.
  • Few large organizations such as Microsoft and Apple have already made commitments to be net-zero by 2030.
  • Cloud computing will dominate the data industry.
